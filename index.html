AURA

European Origin Proof Standard

Open standard for verifiable proof of origin for human, hybrid and AI-generated works.

GitHub Specification (Draft) Public Draft v0.1 (coming soon)

What is AURA?

AURA is an open standard that defines how to declare and verify the origin of digital assets (audio, video, image, text, datasets, AI outputs) in a way that is cryptographically verifiable and interoperable across platforms and institutions.

Scope & Principles

  • Origin proof at the moment of creation, not after the fact.
  • Cryptographic integrity (hash) and verifiable timestamps.
  • Separation between the standard (AURA) and implementations (services, products).
  • Alignment with European frameworks (AI Act, TDM opt-out, copyright).

What AURA does (and does not do)

Does

  • Provide a structured manifest for origin, rights and integrity.
  • Enable independent verification of origin and integrity.
  • Expose machine-readable signals for AI training and TDM rights.

Does not

  • No content recognition or fingerprinting database.
  • No DRM, no playback control.
  • No decision-making on licensing terms or remuneration.

Governance

AURA is published as an open Public Draft. The objective is to converge towards formal standardization with an independent body (e.g. European or international standardization organizations).

Implementations (such as proof-of-origin services) remain fully independent and interoperable as long as they comply with the AURA specification.

Why Europe needs AURA

The European Union holds specific competences in the field of culture, copyright, audiovisual creation and the protection of cultural diversity. These competences include support for cross-border cooperation, transparency of rights management and the protection of creators in the digital environment.

With the emergence of artificial intelligence, generative systems and large-scale content transformation, the question of origin, authorship and lawful use of works has become a strategic issue for Europe.

AURA is designed as a European-compatible open standard that enables:

  • Verifiable proof of origin for human, hybrid and AI-generated works.
  • Machine-readable transparency signals for AI training and TDM rights.
  • Interoperability between platforms, collecting societies and public institutions.
  • Auditability and legal traceability across borders.

AURA does not replace national or European regulations. It provides a technical and legal interoperability layer compatible with existing European frameworks, including copyright law, transparency obligations and emerging AI regulation.

License

The AURA standard is released under the Apache License 2.0, a permissive open-source license suitable for institutional and industrial adoption.
