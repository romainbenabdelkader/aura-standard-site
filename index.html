AURA ==== Open Standard for Verifiable Proof of Origin (European-compatible) Public Draft v0.1 — Specification coming soon AURA is an open standard that defines how to declare and verify the origin of digital assets (audio, video, image, text, datasets, AI outputs) in a way that is cryptographically verifiable and interoperable across platforms and institutions. --- What is AURA? ------------- AURA provides a neutral, open and machine-readable framework for: - Declaring the origin of digital works at the moment of creation - Verifying integrity and provenance independently - Exposing transparent signals for lawful use, including AI training and TDM rights AURA is designed as an infrastructure layer for trust, not as a product, service or platform. --- Scope & Principles ------------------ - Proof of origin at creation time, not after the fact - Cryptographic integrity (hash-based verification) - Verifiable timestamps - Separation between the standard (AURA) and implementations (services, products, platforms) - Alignment with European regulatory frameworks, including AI Act, TDM opt-out and copyright --- What AURA does (and does not do) -------------------------------- ✅ **AURA does** - Provide a structured manifest for origin, rights and integrity - Enable independent verification of origin and integrity - Expose machine-readable transparency signals for AI training and TDM rights ❌ **AURA does not** - No content recognition or fingerprinting database - No DRM, no playback or usage control - No decision-making on licensing terms or remuneration AURA defines how provenance is declared and verified, not how content is monetized or controlled. --- Governance ---------- AURA is published as an open Public Draft. Its objective is to converge toward formal standardization with an independent standardization body (European or international). Implementations (such as proof-of-origin services) remain fully independent and interoperable, as long as they comply with the AURA specification. --- Why Europe needs AURA --------------------- The European Union holds specific competences in the fields of: - Culture - Copyright - Audiovisual creation - Protection of cultural diversity With the emergence of artificial intelligence, generative systems and large-scale content transformation, the questions of origin, authorship and lawful use of works have become strategic issues for Europe. AURA is designed as a European-compatible open standard that enables: - Verifiable proof of origin for human, hybrid and AI-generated works - Machine-readable transparency signals for AI training and TDM rights - Interoperability between platforms, collecting societies and public institutions - Auditability and legal traceability across borders AURA does not replace national or European regulations. It provides a technical and legal interoperability layer compatible with existing European frameworks, including: - Copyright law - Transparency obligations - Emerging AI regulation --- License ------- The AURA standard is released under the Apache License 2.0, a permissive open-source license suitable for institutional and industrial adoption. --- Get involved ------------ The work on AURA is iterative.
